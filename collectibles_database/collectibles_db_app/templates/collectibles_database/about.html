Item Types

  • Circulation Coins

    Circulation coins are coins issued by a state. They can be used to pay at any time without a problem - provided that they aren't old coins that lost their status as legal tender.

  • Banknotes

    A banknote is a type of negotiable promissory note, made by a bank or other licensed authority, payable to the bearer on demand.

  • Commemorative Coins

    A commemorative coin is a coin issued to commemorate some particular event or issue with a distinct design with reference to the occasion on which they were issued.

    Many coins of this category serve as collector's items only, although some countries also issue commemorative coins for regular circulation.

  • Circulating Commemoratives

    Circulating commemoratives are intended to be used for commerce, but the design will only be issued for a limited time to commemorate an event, anniversary, person or location, among other items.

    Examples include the â‚¬2 commemorative coins and U.S. 50 State Quarters.

  • Collector Coins

    Collector coins, also known as numismatic coins, are specially minted coins that are sought after and collected for their rarity, historical significance, artistic design, or other unique qualities.

    Unlike regular circulation coins used in everyday transactions, collector coins are primarily intended for numismatists, coin enthusiasts, and collectors.

  • Bullion Coins

    Bullion coins are a specific type of coin that is primarily valued for its precious metal content rather than its rarity or numismatic qualities.

  • Medals

    Medals are a type of coin-like objects that are distinct from traditional currency coins.

    They do not have a face value, no issuing nation and can be produced and distributed by any person or institution

  • Other

    These may be other items that do not fit into any of the categories listed above.
